For sellers

Four things that make
clothing sell fast.

01

Photos matter more than any other category

Good lighting, flat lay or worn, multiple angles. Front, back, close-up of any wear or flaws. Buyers can't touch it — your photos have to do that job.

02

List your measurements alongside the size label

Sizing varies wildly by brand. An "M" at one brand is an "L" at another. Include chest, waist, and length measurements — it eliminates returns and disputes.

04

Price to sell

Clothing moves fast when priced right. Price too high and it sits. Price it at 30–50% of retail and expect a message within a day. You can always negotiate up if there's strong interest.

03

Be honest about condition

Mention any pilling, fading, or alterations. A buyer who knows what they're getting rarely has complaints. A buyer who was surprised always does.

Clothing is the best category for trades.

Swap a jacket for a jacket, a formal dress for a formal dress — no money changes hands, zero fee. Mark your listing as "open to trade" and describe what you're looking for in the description. Someone at your school probably has exactly what you want.
