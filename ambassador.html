Launch Loot at your school

Become a campus
ambassador.

We're looking for one student per campus to lead Loot's launch. If you know your school, this is for you.

What you do

You own your
campus launch.

This isn't a brand rep role where you hand out flyers. You're the operator. You decide how Loot lands at your school.

01

Own your campus launch

You're the face of Loot at your school. You organise the launch, spread the word, and make sure Loot has supply (sellers) and demand (buyers) from day one.

02

Build the supply

Reach out to student groups, course union reps, and residence councils. Get students to post their first listings before launch day so buyers find real inventory immediately.

03

Drive signups

Flyers, Instagram posts, Reddit threads (r/[yourschool]), Discord servers, Facebook student groups, class announcements, word of mouth — whatever works on your campus. Online reach matters as much as physical presence. Make sure every student has heard of Loot before they need to buy a textbook.

04

Be the feedback loop

You're our eyes and ears on campus. What's working, what isn't, what students are asking for. Direct line to the founding team — your input shapes what we build next.

What you get

Real upside. Real experience.

Founding Ambassador badge — permanent

Your Loot profile gets a "Founding Ambassador" badge that stays forever. You were there from the start — that's visible to every future user at your school.

Revenue share on your campus

You earn a percentage of transactions at your school. Details are set on application and scale with volume. The more your campus transacts, the more you earn.

Direct line to the founding team

Slack channel, weekly check-ins, and a direct line to the people building the product. Your feedback is taken seriously — ambassadors have already influenced core features.

Reference letter from the founding team

Run a successful campus launch and we'll write you a reference letter for jobs, grad school, or anything else. Real startup experience on your resume, backed by real results.

Who we're looking for

Not a marketing role.
An operator role.

No business background required.

You don't need a marketing degree or a big following. You need to know your campus, care about the problem, and be the kind of person who actually does what they say they'll do. One ambassador per school. Starting in Ontario, expanding across Canada.

You know your campus

You know which student groups matter, which buildings everyone hangs out in, and how information actually spreads at your school.

You get things done

You follow up on your own, don't need hand-holding, and care about the outcome — not just the title.
