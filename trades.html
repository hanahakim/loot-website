How it works

Three steps.
Zero fees.

01

Mark your listing as open to trade

When posting, select "Trade" or "Open to trade" and describe what you're looking for. Be specific — "looking for ECON 2G03 textbook" is better than "looking for textbooks."

02

A student proposes their item

Another student sees your listing and proposes their item in the app chat. You review their offer, ask questions, check their rating. You're in control of whether to proceed.

03

Agree, meet, swap — done

You agree on the swap, pick a campus meetup spot, inspect both items, and exchange. Rate each other after. Zero fees on the transaction — Loot's 5% fee only applies to in-app cash payments.

Why it works

The safest trade
environment you'll find.

Both parties have something on the line.

When you're both giving something up, people actually show up. Verified students, real accounts with ratings and transaction history, real consequences for flaking. The skin-in-the-game nature of trades means they have a higher show-up rate than cash transactions — and a lower dispute rate.

Trade tips

Four things that make
trades go smoothly.

  • Be specific in your listing. "Looking to trade for ECON 2G03 (any edition post-2020, good condition)" is actionable. "Open to trades" is not.
  • Agree on condition expectations for both items before meeting. What does "good condition" mean for your item? What do you expect from theirs? Agree in the chat before you travel to the meetup.
  • Inspect both items at the same time. Don't hand over your item before checking theirs. Inspect simultaneously — you both see each other's reaction in real time.
  • Rate each other after. Even trades get ratings. A student with a 4.9 rating and 12 completed trades is a much safer swap than someone brand new. Build your reputation — it pays off.

Trades are completely free.

No 5% fee. No payment processing. Loot's service fee only applies to cash transactions processed through in-app payments. When you're just swapping stuff — no money changes hands — there's nothing to charge a fee on. On us.
